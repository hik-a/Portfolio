Digital Twin Pilot – DOW

Developing a sophisticated digital replica for hospital infrastructure to monitor real-time environmental data and optimize facilities management.

Executive Summary

Designed and documented a Digital Twin pilot for DOW University to monitor critical server room environments. The project bridged IoT hardware with a virtual representation to provide real-time visibility into power consumption, temperature, and human activity.

Pilot Strategy

Implementation Phases

  • Hardware Ingestion: Sensor deployment and network calibration.
  • Twin Modeling: Mapping physical assets to digital logic layers (DTDL).
  • Observation: Two-week monitoring and performance evaluation period.
  • Reporting: Data analysis and strategic scaling recommendations.

Business Value

  • Improved operational visibility and incident response time.
  • Significant reduction in risk of critical infrastructure failure.
  • Energy optimization and sustainability insights for management.
  • Scalable foundation for intelligent, integrated hospital infrastructure.

Functional Capabilities

  • Real-time dashboards for environmental and power metrics.
  • Configurable alerts and automated emergency notifications.
  • Historical data analysis and trend reporting for energy usage.
  • Role-based access control for IT and facility maintenance teams.
  • Predictive maintenance insights based on sensor deviations.
