ISO 9001:2015 Supply Chain Quality Control

PR.005 – Procurement Process

Standard Operating Procedure defining an ISO 9001 compliant end-to-end global supply chain and quality control framework.

Standardization Objective

Developed and maintained a comprehensive procurement framework to ensure material availability, cost optimization, and full compliance with ISO 9001:2015 quality standards.

This process governs everything from initial vendor selection and evaluation to incoming quality inspections and performance monitoring.

Lifecycle Stages

1. Requisition & Approval

  • Generation of Purchase Requisitions (PR) via ERP.
  • Budget verification and tiered managerial approvals.

2. Sourcing & Selection

  • Floating RFQs/RFPs to the Approved Vendor List (AVL).
  • Conducting Technical & Commercial Bid Evaluations.

3. Vendor Evaluation (ISO 8.4)

  • Periodic desktop and site audits for critical suppliers.
  • Review of quality certifications and financial stability.

4. PO Issuance

  • Generation of Purchase Orders with standardized T&Cs.
  • Lead-time commitment tracking.

5. Production & Expediting

  • Real-time tracking of high-priority orders.
  • Weekly status reports and critical path management.

6. Logistics & Customs

  • Transportation coordination and global customs compliance.
  • Management of shipping documents (Invoices, PL).

7. Incoming Quality (IQC)

  • Warehouse receipt verification and physical inspection.
  • Handling Non-Conformance Reports (NCR) and CAPA.

8. Performance Monitoring

  • Quarterly KPI evaluation (delivery, quality).
  • Strategic probation for non-performers.

Risk & Compliance Protocols

  • Currency Risk: Protocols for handling currency fluctuation in international orders.
  • Environmental: Strict adherence to RoHS and REACH standards for material sourcing.
  • Record Retention: 7-year retention policy for all procurement audit trails per statutory requirements.
